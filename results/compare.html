Side-by-side Response · Q.03 — Critical Thinking
Question: "You are given five hundred dollars to improve the experience of incoming first-years. Take me through what you would choose to do."
Hiroshi
"I would begin by ranking pain points by frequency multiplied by intensity. With five hundred dollars I would select whichever lever produces the greatest expected reduction per dollar — most likely a peer-mentor onboarding programme, since the binding constraint here is loneliness, not information."
Aanya
"To begin, I would not commit the budget without first understanding the actual difficulties. I would speak to incoming students first, then split the budget between a small orientation kit and a peer-led mentorship programme — but if forced to choose, I would retain the mentorship programme: it pays compound returns through the year."
Priya
"I would organise a welcome week with curated mentor sessions. The five hundred dollars would cover refreshments and printed handbooks. The objective is to make sure every first-year leaves the first week knowing two senior students by name."
